Power Macintosh G4 (Gigabit Ethernet)

Specifications

  • Processor: 2x PowerPC G4 @ 450 MHz
  • RAM: 512MB PC100 (expandable up to a maximum of 2GB, or 1.5GB if using Mac OS 9)
  • Video card: ATI Rage 128 Pro AGP
  • Operating system: Mac OS 9.2.1 + Mac OS X 10.3
  • Other expansions: Matrox RTMac, PCI FireWire card, PCI USB 2.0 + FireWire Card, AirPort card

About this computer

The Gigabit Ethernet version of the PowerMac G4 added, as the name implies, a Gigabit Ethernet port. However, that model also added the option of ordering the machine with not just one, but TWO PowerPC 7400 (G4) processors, and the new ADC port, which carried power, USB and video signals to your monitor all on one cable (though it failed terribly).

My specific PowerMac was probably purchased by someone looking to do video editing on it, seeing that it came with a second FireWire card and the Matrox RTMac.

The Matrox RTMac

The Matrox RTMac was a very powerful PCI-X card designed for people looking to edit video on their G4 system. It let users connect a second display, capture video using an external video I/O connection box, and speed up certain video editing programs.
