Rewireable plugs

Type L plugs

These are some newer rewireable plugs, not really noteworthy on their own, but it's interesting to compare them to the plug from the 50s shown in the other pages (escpecially for people who aren't familiar with how Italian plugs look like).

The plugs in the first picture are all 10A type-L (Italian) plugs, some of them with an earth pin and some of them without it, whereas the plug shown in the second picture is of the 16A type, which is slightly more uncommon but it's definitely not rare, they're generally used on power strips. I don't have any rewireable Schuko plugs to show here yet, otherwise I'd post a picture of one of them too, but if you're really interested in them I'm sure you can find some pictures online.

Schuko plugs

Standard European Schuko plugs are also generally available in hardware stores, although the rewireable ones aren't all that common as they'd probably require an adaptor, so if you need to install a plug yourself it generally makes sense to use a type L one instead.
Schuko plugs are, however, much more common on power cords that come with appliances, with the plug generally moulded on at the factory.

Modern Italian sockets

Some examples of modern Italian sockets, from the Vimar Plana series (a very popular series nowadays).
The second one is an italianised Schuko socket, which means that apart from accepting Schuko plugs it can also be used with 10A and 16A type L plugs, since it has the central earth hole and the holes are of the correct spacing.

Adaptors

Schuko adaptors

As mentioned in the introductory page, Italy uses both type L and European Schuko plugs, despite the fact that most houses only have sockets that accept type L plugs and not Schuko ones.
Therefore, adaptors are generally used, or a power strip that has Schuko-compatible sockets (most modern ones do).

These devices are always rated for 1500W maximum, although they're often also used for things like washing machines and hairdryers.

Additionally, adaptors that do the opposite thing also exist, they are less common but they're useful to connect type L plugs to "normal" Schuko sockets.

Type L to Type L adaptors

Since older houses had less sockets, and those sockets often weren't of the right type, adaptors like these were/are used quite a lot. I've also seen these used with Schuko plugs jammed into them, which is very unsafe and can break the adaptor.
You can see very clearly, in the second picture, that the earth pin on that adaptor has been cut off. This was because many older sockets didn't have an earth hole, so adaptors like these wouldn't fit.
